## Where PagoPay Wins

### Zero Foreign Exchange Fees

This is PagoPay's biggest advantage for international users. Every transaction in a non-USD currency costs 0% in FX fees on both the Plastic and Metal cards. Coinbase charges 2% on every international transaction outside the US. For someone spending $2,000/month abroad, that's $480/year saved with PagoPay.

### No Bank Account Required

PagoPay only requires identity verification (KYC) to get started. Coinbase requires a full Coinbase exchange account with a linked bank account. This makes PagoPay significantly more accessible in regions where traditional banking is limited — particularly Latin America and Africa, where PagoPay has dedicated regional products.

### Higher Daily Spending Limits

PagoPay supports $100,000/day in POS spending on both card tiers. Coinbase Card limits vary and are generally lower, especially for newer accounts.

### Premium Metal Card with Luxury Perks

PagoPay's Metal Card ($500 for 2 years, no monthly fee, 2.5% add-funds rate) includes access to a Luxury Marketplace, Private Jet booking, Exclusive Member Events, and 24/7 Concierge Support. Coinbase offers no premium tier or lifestyle perks.

### Broader Geographic Coverage

PagoPay works in 160+ countries with purpose-built experiences for Latin America and Africa. Coinbase Card is limited to the US, parts of Europe, and the UK.

---

## Where Coinbase Card Wins

### No Card Issuance Fee

Coinbase Card is free to issue with no monthly fee. PagoPay's Plastic Card costs $19.99 upfront plus $3.50/month (first month free). However, the Metal Card's $500 upfront covers two full years with no monthly fee, which may be more cost-effective for regular users.

### Crypto Cashback Rewards

Coinbase offers up to 4% cashback in crypto on purchases. PagoPay's rewards program is not yet available. For users who prioritize earning crypto on spending, Coinbase currently has the edge.

### Established Brand and Liquidity

Coinbase is a publicly traded company (NASDAQ: COIN) with deep liquidity and a large user base. Users already on the Coinbase exchange can add a card with minimal friction. PagoPay is a newer platform in early access rollout.

### Lower Add-Funds Spread (Marginal)

Coinbase charges a 2.49% spread on conversions, compared to PagoPay's 3% (Plastic) or 2.5% (Metal). The difference is small — and for international users, PagoPay's zero FX fees more than offset the slightly higher conversion rate.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is PagoPay cheaper than Coinbase Card?**
It depends on usage. For international spending, PagoPay is cheaper because of its zero FX fees. For US-only domestic spending, Coinbase's free card and slightly lower spread make it more affordable. The PagoPay Metal Card is the best value for regular users who want premium perks without a monthly fee.

**Can I use Coinbase Card outside the US?**
Coinbase Card works in parts of Europe and the UK, but charges a 2% international transaction fee. PagoPay works in 160+ countries with zero FX fees.

**Does PagoPay have crypto rewards?**
PagoPay's rewards program is coming soon. Currently, Coinbase offers up to 4% cashback in crypto.

**Which card has better security?**
Both use industry-standard security. PagoPay uses AES-256 encryption, 3DS Secure, and regulated custody through ALT 5 Sigma. Coinbase uses its vault technology and two-factor authentication. PagoPay is FINTRAC-registered in Canada; Coinbase holds state licenses in the US.
