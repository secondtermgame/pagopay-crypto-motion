## Tips for Spending Bitcoin Efficiently

**Watch the conversion timing.** Your BTC converts to USD at the moment of deposit, not at the moment of purchase. If you believe Bitcoin's price will rise, you might prefer to deposit in smaller amounts closer to when you plan to spend.

**Use stablecoins for predictable spending.** If you want to avoid price volatility between deposit and spending, deposit USDT or USDC instead of BTC. They maintain a 1:1 peg to USD, so $100 deposited equals $100 available.

**Track for taxes.** In most jurisdictions, spending Bitcoin is a taxable event — the conversion from BTC to fiat creates a capital gain or loss. Keep records of your deposits and the BTC price at the time of conversion for tax reporting.

**Consider the Metal Card for regular use.** If you spend $500+/month in crypto, the Metal Card's lower 2.5% add-funds rate (vs. 3% on Plastic) saves you money over time, and the $500 covers two full years with no monthly fee.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do merchants know I'm paying with Bitcoin?**
No. The merchant receives a normal card payment in their local currency. The Bitcoin-to-USD conversion happens on PagoPay's side before the payment reaches the merchant.

**How fast is the conversion?**
On PagoPay, Bitcoin deposits convert to USD instantly once confirmed on the blockchain. Bitcoin network confirmations typically take 10–30 minutes.

**Can I spend Bitcoin internationally?**
Yes. PagoPay cards work in 160+ countries with zero foreign exchange fees. You can spend in any local currency — the conversion is handled automatically.

**Is spending Bitcoin taxable?**
In most countries, converting Bitcoin to fiat is a taxable event that may result in a capital gain or loss. Consult a tax professional for advice specific to your jurisdiction.

**What if Bitcoin's price drops after I deposit?**
Since PagoPay converts BTC to USD at the time of deposit, your USD balance is locked in at that rate. Subsequent Bitcoin price movements don't affect your card balance. If you want to avoid this risk entirely, deposit stablecoins (USDT, USDC) instead.
