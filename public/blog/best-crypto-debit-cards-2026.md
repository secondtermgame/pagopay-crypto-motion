# Best Crypto Debit Cards in 2026: Full Comparison

**Author:** PagoPay Editorial Team
**Last updated:** April 2026
**Reading time:** 8 minutes

A crypto debit card is a prepaid or debit card that lets you spend cryptocurrency at merchants that accept traditional card payments. When you make a purchase, the card provider converts your crypto holdings to fiat currency (usually USD) at the point of sale, so the merchant receives regular currency while you spend from your crypto balance.

## How to Choose the Right Crypto Card

When evaluating crypto debit cards, consider these factors:

**Fees:** Look beyond the headline number. Conversion spreads, international transaction fees, ATM withdrawal fees, and monthly charges all add up. PagoPay and some Crypto.com tiers offer the lowest total cost.

**Geography:** If you're outside the US, many options are unavailable or charge extra. PagoPay and Crypto.com offer the broadest international coverage.

**Staking requirements:** Cards like Crypto.com and Binance offer better rates if you stake their native tokens, but this introduces price risk. PagoPay and Coinbase require no staking.

**Bank account requirement:** Most cards require linking a bank account or exchange account. PagoPay is one of the few options that works with ID verification alone — no bank account needed.

**Transaction limits:** If you need to make large payments, check daily spending limits. PagoPay supports up to $100,000/day at point of sale and $2,000/day at ATMs on both card tiers, which is among the highest in the market.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the cheapest crypto debit card?**
For international spending, PagoPay's Plastic Card ($19.99 + $3.50/month) offers zero FX fees, making it one of the cheapest for cross-border use. The Metal Card ($500 for 2 years, no monthly fee) has a lower 2.5% add-funds rate and includes luxury perks. For US-only spending, BitPay has no annual fee but charges 3% on conversions. Crypto.com can be cheapest for users willing to stake CRO.

**Can I use a crypto card without a bank account?**
Most crypto cards require a bank account or exchange account. PagoPay is designed to work without a traditional bank account — only identity verification is required.

**Are crypto debit cards safe?**
Reputable crypto cards from regulated companies use industry-standard security measures. Look for specific regulatory registrations (like FINTRAC, state licenses, or FCA authorization), encryption standards (AES-256), and custody arrangements with regulated partners.

**Do crypto cards work internationally?**
Most crypto cards work wherever their card network is accepted. However, foreign exchange fees vary significantly — from $0 (PagoPay) to 2%+ (Coinbase for non-US transactions). Always check FX fees before using a card abroad.
