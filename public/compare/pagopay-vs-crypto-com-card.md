## Where PagoPay tends to win

### Zero FX fees on international card spend

PagoPay markets **no foreign exchange fees** on international purchases with its USD-denominated prepaid card. For frequent travelers or cross-border spenders, that positioning is easy to understand and compare.

### Straight tier story: Plastic vs Metal

Two clear card economics (Plastic vs Metal) with published card price, monthly fee rules, and load commission bands — see our [pricing page](https://mypagopay.com/pricing).

### Emerging-market positioning

PagoPay highlights **Latin America** and **Africa** landing experiences and KYC-first onboarding **without** requiring a traditional bank rail for the core story.

### High published POS ceiling

**$100,000/day POS** (as stated in PagoPay marketing materials) is a simple headline for high-volume spenders comparing limits.

## Where Crypto.com tends to win

### Deep app ecosystem

Crypto.com pairs cards with exchange, earn, and other products in one brand — attractive if you already live inside that ecosystem.

### Rewards and lounge access (tier/region dependent)

On qualifying tiers and regions, perks such as cashback in crypto or lounge access can outweigh raw FX savings for some users.

### Brand scale

Crypto.com is a globally recognized consumer crypto brand with broad card program history in supported markets.

## Cost intuition (not financial advice)

- **Heavy international spend:** compare **published international transaction / FX policies** side by side; PagoPay’s **$0 FX fee** pitch is the variable to model first.
- **Domestic-only, ecosystem user:** Crypto.com may win on integrated rewards if you stake and use supported products in your region.
- **No bank account / remittance-style use cases:** evaluate whether each provider supports your country and onboarding path; PagoPay explicitly markets **LATAM** and **Africa** experiences.
